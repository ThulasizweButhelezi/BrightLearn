BrightTV Case Study – Viewership Analytics
Overview
BrightTV’s CEO set a goal to grow the company’s subscription base for the financial year. The CVM (Customer Value Management) team needed insights into user behavior, viewing trends, and growth opportunities.
This case study was prepared to answer four key questions:
1. User and usage trends – Who watches BrightTV and how engaged are they?
2. Factors influencing consumption – What drives viewing time and engagement?
3. Low-consumption days – Which days and times are weak, and what content can fix them?
4. Growth initiatives – What strategies can convert, retain, and expand the subscriber base?
---
 Data Source
• The dataset was provided by BrightLearn in Excel format.
• It contained user profiles and viewing session records.
• Each session represented one viewing record per subscriber.
• Dates and times were supplied in UTC and converted to South Africa Standard Time for analysis.
---
 Data Processing Workflow
1. Data ingestion – The Excel dataset was imported into Databricks.
2. Data manipulation – SQL scripts were used in Databricks to clean, transform, and analyze the data.
3. Summarization – Key metrics and aggregates were exported back into Excel.
4. Visualization – Charts, heatmaps, and dashboards were built in Excel and PowerPoint to present insights.
---
 Key Insights from the Presentation
• Engagement is the bottleneck: 37.5% of sessions had no viewing; average watch time was only 8.7 minutes.
• Audience concentration: 87% of viewers are male, mostly young adults in Gauteng and Western Cape. Women, children, teens, and older adults are underrepresented.
• Content drivers: Live sport and music deliver 3–4× more watch time than kids or lifestyle channels.
• Time drivers: Prime time sessions are twice as long as morning sessions; weekends contribute 30% of sessions with longer durations.
• Peaks vs weak spots:
	◦ Peaks → Friday/Saturday prime time, Sunday afternoons (sports).
	◦ Weak spots → Monday mornings, mid-week late nights, weekday mornings.
• Quiet pockets: Female 35+ audience is underserved across all dayparts.
• Growth initiatives:
	◦ Convert → free trials, sports bundles, referral campaigns.
	◦ Retain → personalized notifications, win-back journeys, loyalty rewards.
	◦ Expand → female-focused bundles, family/kids plans, provincial expansion.
